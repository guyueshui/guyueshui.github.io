Matrix Factorization

January 3, 2019
Notes
Preliminaries Def: A matrix $A \in M_n$ is normal if $AA^∗ = A^∗A$, that is, if $A$ commutes with its conjugate transpose. Def: A complex matrix $A$ is unitary if $AA^∗ = I$ or $A^∗A = I$, and a real matrix $B$ is orthogonal if $BB^T = I$ or $B^TB = I$. There is no so-called “orthonormal” matrix. There is just an orthogonal matrix whose rows or columns are orthonormal vectors.

Exponential Distribution

December 25, 2018
Notes
Story The Exponential distribution is the continuous counterpart to the Geometric distribution. The story of the Exponential distribution is analogous, but we are now waiting for a success in continuous time, where successes arrive at a rate of $\lambda$ successes per unit of time. The average number of successes in a time interval of length $t$ is $\lambda t$, though the actual number of successes varies randomly. An Exponential random variable represents the waiting time until the first arrival of a success.
